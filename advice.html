Prevention of exposure to rabies


1-Avoid contact with wild animals

2-Avoid contact with stray dogs and cats

3-Check whether your pet is vaccinated against rabies as required by law

4-Avoid contact with unfamiliar pets unless confirmed by their owners and after checking that they were vaccinated against rabies

5-Note abonormal behavior in domestic animals and livestock. If an animal shows any sign of abnormal behavior, contact the municipal veterinary service

6-If you witness aggressive animal behavior or find bite marks on animals either belonging to you or not, contact the municipal veterinary service immediately

Emergency room treatment for bites


1-Local treatment of the wound depending on the type of wound.

2-Immediate stitching of the wound should be avoided, unless facial. If the doctor decides to go ahead and stitch the wound anyway, contact your Health Center doctor first to find out

 whether administration of a passive vaccination is advised. If so, it must

 be applied to the wound and the surrounding area before stitching. Stitch only after wound is clean; stitching must be limited to seaming the lips of the wound only.

3-The doctor will consider prescribing antibiotics.

4-The doctor will consider whether an anti-tetanus injection is required.

5-The doctor at the emergency ward should call and consult the health center physician on duty as to whether administration of preventive rabies injections is necessary.

6-The attending physician will refer such patients to the health center for further treatment. If the center is closed, the patient can be referred to the health center in the following business day.

Information

deases that can be transmitted from animals to human :

1- Toxoplasmosis

One of the most common diseases that can be transmitted from cats to humans is toxoplasmosis, toxic plasma disease, or Toxoplasmosis. Toxoplasma is one of the

 most common and common parasites in cats, which can be found in feces of cats and then can be transmitted to humans. Although this disease does not represent a significant risk for

 humans in general, but it is one of the most dangerous diseases for pregnant women .. In the event of this disease during pregnancy, it may cause serious complications for the fetus and

its growth. Therefore, pregnant women should be cautious when dealing with cats by washing hands and avoiding handling cat feces.

2-Cat scratch disease

Humans can develop this disease if they are scratched or bitten by cats or licked into open wounds, which leads to the transmission of a bacterial infection to humans causing swelling in

 the affected areas and full of pus, with the possibility of fever and headache. Generally, these symptoms go within a few days, and antibiotics may be required. To avoid this disease, you

 must constantly trim your pet's nails, and not play violently with them.

deases that can be transmitted from human to animal :

1- Parotiditis (mumps)

Mumps is a highly contagious viral disease in humans. The initial symptoms of the disease include lethargy, muscle pain, headache and fever, usually followed by painful swelling of the

 parotid salivary glands, located on both sides of the face. This disease is widely bought among children, but it may nevertheless affect a person of any age and of any gender Also, this

disease is prevented by vaccination with ease. And if a person has mumps, he will recover within a week or two, but in some cases the virus can cause serious complications such as

 hearing loss, pancreatitis, testicles, ovaries and the brain. If the dog becomes infected with this infection, symptoms of the disease are treated so that the body can resist it and thus recover

 from it. Most dogs recover from a mumps infection in five to ten days.

2-Salmonella bacteria

While salmonella is often associated with food poisoning, its transmission to humans is very possible through contact with infected animals, and vice versa. In both humans and dogs,

 salmonella can cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, fever, headache and abdominal cramps. Young children and the elderly are more likely to have complications from Salmonella infection.

 In order to prevent salmonella, you should prevent the dog from drinking from the toilet or messing with litter boxes, especially while walking the dog, so we always recommend that you

 put a protective mask during the dogs' walk. Because salmonella is linked to food, it is necessary to adhere to washing hands continuously, as well as to ensure the safety of food before

 serving to the dog.
